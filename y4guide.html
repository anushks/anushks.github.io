Question Banks

  1. Passmedicine - good for consolidating as you go along throughout the year
  2. Assess and Progress - closest questions to your exams, do later in the year
  3. Pastest - not all SBAs
  4. Quesmed - harder/roguer questions, not exactly in UCL style, but close

Note: UCL really like asking questions in the style of what is the single best diagnostic test, or the most important initial step.

Making the most of going in

  • Go to bedside teaching! If there's something you want them to teach you about, talk to other people in your group and ask the clinician.
  • If you find a clinician who is especially good at teaching, find out when their ward rounds/clinics are and try go to those.
  • Spending time in clinics where you are ignored is a waste of time
  • Ward rounds are either amazing or a waste of time, depending on how keen to teach the clinician is - make yourself useful and ask questions/write in the notes
  • Try not to tread on other students' learning opportunities
  • MOST IMPORTANTLY: every time you go to the wards/clerk, go with a concrete aim e.g. I wanna hear a murmur/examine for signs, or I want to improve my history taking.

Bookwork can get very boring at times - a lot of the time talking to patients with those conditions will give you most of the insight you'll need. One of the best ways to learn is to clerk a patient with a condition and then go read up about it. It is important to manage your time effectively between clerking patients/reading/doing question banks!

Medicine is only interesting if you make it so - good luck and have some fun!
